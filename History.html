Capitals of KZ

The capital was officially moved from Almaty to Akmola in 1997, and in May 1998 the city was renamed Astana, and in 2019 it was renamed Nur-Sultan. Now it is a modern city with a favorable environment attractive for tourists.

Historical Figures of KZ

Abai Kunanbayev

Poet

The heritage he left his nation is rich in songs and poems, translations and prose. His translations of the poetry written by Russian writers and poets such as Pushkin, Lermontov became the national patrimony of Kazakhstan.

Alikhan Bukeikhanov

Statesman

He was a publicist, teacher, writer and environmental scientist who was a founder and led the Alash party. He served as the Prime Minister of the Alash Autonomy from 1917 to 1920, making him the first person in the history of Kazakhstan to hold such a position.

Kanysh Satpayev

Scientist

Kanysh Satpayev was one of the founders of Soviet metallogeny and the principal advocate and first president of Kazakhstan Academy of Sciences. He left after himself more than 640 scientific publications. He created Institute of Geology which became the center of studies of Kazakhstan mineral resources.
