Nur-Sultan is a city of Big Dreams

A little about the capital

Capital of Kazakhstan's favourable location in the centre of the Eurasian continent makes it an economically advantageous transport, communication, logistics and financial center - a natural bridge between Europe and Asia and point of integration into
the global economy for the region.

History

Nur-Sultan (formerly Akmolinsk, Tselinograd, Akmola, Astana) has been the capital of the Republic of Kazakhstan since December 10, 1997. The city is located in the north of the country. The city is located in the center of Kazakhstan. In a short time, the new capital, born by the will of the First President of Kazakhstan, acquired the status of a national idea, became a symbol of Independence and global success of the young state.

Climate

Nur-Sultan is located on the banks of the Ishim River. Due to the remote location from the oceans, winter here is colder than in cities located to the west at the same latitude (Uralsk, Voronezh, London). Summer here is warm and dry, and despite this, most of the precipitation falls during the warm period (April-October). Dust storms are possible in summer.

Population

The city was conceived as an administrative center like Washington, with a population of no more than 500-600 thousand people. However, the increase over 23 years amounted to almost 850 thousand people, and as of May 1, 2021, 1 million 199 thousand 83 residents officially live in Nur-Sultan. It was planned that the capital will become a millionaire by 2030, but this happened 10 years earlier.

Khan Shatyr

The largest tent-shaped building in the world. In the Guinness Book of Records, the Khan Shatyr shopping and entertainment center has also managed to enter - this is the largest
tent-shaped building in the world. The height of this architectural miracle, together with the spire, is 150 meters. Building Architect - Norman Foster.

Akorda

Akorda is a large palace that combines traditional Kazakh, Eastern and European motifs. Akorda is not only the main building of the country, but also a key element that makes up the main architectural composition of the left bank of the capital.In addition, a team of leading Kazakh and foreign architects worked on the basic concept of the building.
