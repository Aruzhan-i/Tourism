About Nur-Sultan

Capital of Kazakhstan's favourable location in the centre of the Eurasian continent makes it an economically advantageous transport, communication, logistics and financial center - a natural bridge between Europe and Asia and point of integration into the global economy for the region.



Climate

Nur-Sultan is the second-coldest national capital in the world after Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia.Nur-Sultan is located on the banks of the Ishim River. Due to the remote location from the oceans, winter here is colder than in cities located to the west at the same latitude (Uralsk, Voronezh, London). Summer here is warm and dry, and despite this, most of the precipitation falls during the warm period (April-October). Dust storms are possible in summer.

History

Nur-Sultan (formerly Akmolinsk, Tselinograd, Akmola, Astana) has been the capital of the Republic of Kazakhstan since December 10, 1997. The city is located in the north of the country. The city is located in the center of Kazakhstan. In a short time, the new capital, born by the will of the First President of Kazakhstan, acquired the status of a national idea, became a symbol of Independence and global success of the young state.

Population

The city was conceived as an administrative center like Washington, with a population of no more than 500-600 thousand people. However, the increase over 23 years amounted to almost 850 thousand people, and as of May 1, 2021, 1 million 199 thousand 83 residents officially live in Nur-Sultan. It was planned that the capital will become a millionaire by 2030, but this happened 10 years earlier.

Khan Shatyr

Khan Shatyr ("Royal Marquee") is a giant transparent tent in Astana, the capital city of Kazakhstan. Built in a distinctively neofuturist style, the architectural project was unveiled by the President of Kazakhstan Nursultan Nazarbayev on December 9, 2006. The largest tent-shaped building in the world. In the Guinness Book of Records, the Khan Shatyr shopping and entertainment center has also managed to enter - this is the largest tent-shaped building in the world. The height of this architectural miracle, together with the spire, is 150 meters. Building Architect - Norman Foster.

Hazrat Sultan Mosque

One of the most modern attractions of Astana, the young Kazakhstan capital, is the Hazrat Sultan Cathedral Mosque. The construction of this largest mosque in Central Asia took as long as 3 years and was inaugurated in July 2012.Hazrat Sultan National Mosque is the largest in Kazakhstan and the second largest in Central Asia.The name of the mosque is translated as "Holy Sultan".So they addressed the poet, thinker and at the same time the Sufi sheikh Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, who lived in the 12th century, after whom it was named.

Akorda

Akorda is not only the main building of the country but also a key element constituting the main architectural composition of the capital's left bank. Akorda is a large palace that combines traditional Kazakh, Eastern and European motifs. Akorda is not only the main building of the country, but also a key element that makes up the main architectural composition of the left bank of the capital.In addition, a team of leading Kazakh and foreign architects worked on the basic concept of the building.
